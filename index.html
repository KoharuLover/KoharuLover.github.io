Building Mental Resilience in Everyday Life

What is Stress?

Stress is the body's natural response to perceived threats or pressure. While short-term (acute) stress can help you stay alert and focused, long-term (chronic) stress can harm both physical and mental health.

  • Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to allow the brain to recharge.
  • Exercise: Even a 20-minute walk boosts mood and brain function.
  • Nutrition: A balanced diet fuels not just your body, but your brain.
    •

    The brain's amygdala (emotions) becomes overactive, while the prefrontal cortex (decision-making) can become less effective—making it harder to think clearly or regulate emotions.

    Why This Matters:

    Understanding how stress works helps us recognize when we're overwhelmed and take steps to restore balance before it affects our health.
